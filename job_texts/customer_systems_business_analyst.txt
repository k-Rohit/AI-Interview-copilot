SUMMARY:
The IT Business Analyst is typically known as the bridge between the business and technology. The successful candidate must be capable of effectively communicating at all levels of the organization. This person must develop strong relationships in order to gain the confidence and respect of our business partners as well as other IT department members in a complex and rapidly changing technical environment.
RESPONSIBILITIES:
Work closely with the business to analyze problem areas and assist in the development of business cases Consults with the business to identify, define and document business needs and objectives,current state procedures, problems, input and output requirements and levels of system access Acts as a liaison between departmental end-users, project manager, information technology team members, consultants and vendors in the analysis, design, configuration, testing and maintenance of information technology systems Analyzes the feasibility of, and develops requirements for, new systems and enhancements to existing systems; ensures the system design fits the needs of the users Tracks and fully documents changes for functional and business specifications; writes detailed universally understood procedures for project artifacts and potential use in training Identifies opportunities for improving business processes through technology and/or non-system changes May assist in the preparation of proposals to develop new systems and/or change existing systems Reads and interprets functional technical literature and translates in terms understandable to the end-users May be required to organize and conduct business process re engineering/improvement sessions May assist in the development of a change management strategy as it relates to information system implementation Leads the system testing process including assisting users with the development of UAT and End-to-End Test plans
